SDG 13 — Climate Action

📚 Key Vocabulary

Weather
noun
The condition of the atmosphere at a specific time and place — rain, sun, wind, or temperature.
"The weather is sunny and warm today."
Climate
noun
The average weather conditions in a place over many years.
"Spain has a warm climate."
Rain
noun
Water that falls from clouds in the sky.
"We need rain for the plants to grow."
Flood
noun
A large amount of water covering an area that is usually dry.
"Heavy rain caused a flood in the city."
Heat
noun
High temperature; extreme warmth from the sun.
"The heat in summer is very strong."
Ice
noun
Frozen water that forms when temperature drops below 0°C.
"The ice on the lake is melting because it's warmer."
Pollution
noun
Harmful chemicals or particles in air, water, or soil.
"Cars and factories cause air pollution."
Forest
noun
A large area of land covered with trees and plants.
"We must protect forests to fight climate change."
